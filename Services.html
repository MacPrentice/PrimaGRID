Maintenance and Repair

  1. Preventive maintenance - Regular maintenance and inspection of equipment to prevent breakdowns and extend the lifespan of the equipment.
  2. Corrective maintenance - Fixing equipment that has broken down or is malfunctioning.
  3. Calibration and testing - Ensuring that equipment is functioning correctly and is accurate.
  4. Emergency repairs - Providing rapid response to unexpected equipment failures to minimize downtime for healthcare facilities.
  5. Equipment upgrades and modernization - Upgrading and modernizing existing equipment to improve efficiency and patient outcomes.
  6. Equipment installation and commissioning - Installing and commissioning new equipment to ensure that it is functioning correctly.
  7. User training - Providing user training for healthcare professionals on how to use and maintain the equipment properly.

Installation

  1. Initial installation - Installing new equipment for healthcare facilities.
  2. Replacement installation - Replacing existing equipment that is no longer functioning or needs to be upgraded.
  3. Relocation installation - Moving equipment from one location to another, such as when healthcare facilities are undergoing renovations.
  4. Retrofit installation - Upgrading and modernizing existing equipment to improve efficiency and patient outcomes.
  5. Compliance installation - Ensuring that the equipment is installed and configured to meet all necessary regulations and standards.
  6. Custom installation - Designing and installing equipment that is customized to meet the specific needs of the healthcare facility.
  7. Testing and commissioning - Ensuring that the equipment is functioning correctly and is compliant with all necessary regulations.
